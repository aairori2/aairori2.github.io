About Me

Analytical. Global.
Data-Driven.

I am Aldi J. Airori, an environmental scientist and Research Technologist I at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln Institute of Agriculture and Natural Resources (IANR), with 4+ years of experience in environmental data analysis, energy research, and sustainable resource management.

My work centers on digital soil carbon research and agroecosystem science — combining field data collection, laboratory analysis, and spatial/statistical modeling in Python, R, and ArcGIS to support emission-reduction strategies and sustainable land management.

I hold an M.S. in Natural Resource Sciences and a B.S. in Environmental Science, both from UNL. My international experience includes work as a Remote Research Consultant for the World Resources Institute, analyzing sustainable energy and agricultural policy across Southeast Asia in coordination with NGOs and government stakeholders.

I am committed to open-access science and reproducible workflows — building public tools and datasets that help accelerate the transition toward a low-carbon global economy.
